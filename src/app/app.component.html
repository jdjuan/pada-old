P.A.D.A.

Purpose | Agenda | Documents | Actions

A standard for better meeting invites:

1. Purpose

Explain what's the expected outcome of the meeting

Every meeting should have a well-defined goal, otherwise, you will end up with wandering conversations.

2. Agenda

List every topic that needs to be addressed

A well-defined set of points makes meetings more organized, allowing you to know where you are at, what's the next topic, and how much is left to be discussed.

3. Documents

Attach relevant documents to be reviewed beforehand

Meetings should be aimed at discussions, not presentations. Send PowerPoint, Word, or Excel files in the meeting invite, and use the allocated time to discuss them.

4. Actions

Highligh tasks attendees are expected to carry out before the session

Meetings are more productive when participants take an active role. Ask them to bring suggestions to the matter that will be discussed.
